Material Risks of Loss

Investing in securities involves a significant risk of loss that clients must be prepared to bear. No investment strategy can guarantee a profit or protect completely against down-market realities.

Key Investment Risk Definitions

  • Market Risk: The underlying value of portfolio investments may decline due to systemic economic shifts or broad negative market sentiment.
  • Equity Risk: Individual stock prices can fluctuate unpredictably and significantly based on target company-specific news, updates, or volatile industry trends.
  • Values-Aligned Screening Risk: By excluding certain sectors, industries, or specific companies for ethical reasons, an investment portfolio may be systematically less diversified than traditional standard benchmarks. This restriction may cause the portfolio to underperform market indices or experience higher localized volatility during distinct economic market cycles.
  • Inflation Risk: The active operational risk that the actual purchasing power of your accumulated assets will not keep pace with the rising costs of traditional goods and services.
  • Liquidity Risk: Specialized ethical allocations or private business investments may be structurally difficult to exit or liquidate quickly without incurring a significant, adverse impact on execution price.
